Burien 4th of July Parade

Date: July 4, 2026

Location: Burien, Washington

Highline Amateur Radio Club is planning to take part in the Burien 4th of July Parade as a club activity.

The day started at 8:00 AM when the communications truck, an M35A2C 2.5-ton cargo truck, arrived on site. By 8:15 AM, the team met up with Discover Burien, who provided a pop-up tent, table, and chairs for the club setup. Between 8:30 and 9:30 AM, volunteers including Mark McCooey, Glen Sankey, Joe Levorsen and his wife, Gerrie Kellog, and Brian Stapleton arrived and helped get everything in place. The group set up the pop-up, table, cabling, and base radios, getting the station ready for parade operations.

Between 9:45 and 10:00 AM, staff provided larger-print order-of-march handouts, and the team sorted out coverage assignments. Gerrie and Brian handled base station duties, while the hour before the start was spent helping coordinate and keep participants moving into position. The parade rolled out on time at 11:00 AM, the route was cleared into the breakdown area by 12:15 PM, and the equipment was stowed and secured for the trip home by 1:00 PM.
